About the SR7500 Model 14

This SR7500 has the following capabilities:

  • Read cards with magnetic strips or integrated circuit (IC) chips, and write information to the cards.
  • Issue receipts
  • Dispense cash
  • Accept cash deposits, without deposit slips or envelopes, and credit the proper amount to the account
  • Recycle accepted cash for use in dispensing cash
  • (Optional) Write in passbooks, and print entire passbooks
  • (Optional) Print a journal for bank use that records all SR7500 actions

There are two types of SR7500 Model 14 A, Front Type and Rear Type. The Front Type SR7500 provides operator access to internal components from the same side as the Operation Panel. The Rear Type SR7500 provides operator access to internal components from the side opposite the Operation Panel. See See SR7500 Appearance and Parts Guide for more information.

The type name for SR7500 Model 14 is “TS-EA45-I34xxxx (Rear Type)” or “TS-EA45-I44xxxx (Front Type)”. “xxxx” represents alphanumeric characters.

Preparing to Insert Banknotes into a Cash Box

Before inserting banknotes into a Cash Box, you must prepare the banknotes for insertion. This ensures that banknotes inside the Cash Box can be moved properly. The preparation for inserting banknotes into a Cash Box is different for mint (new) banknotes and used banknotes.

Preparing Mint Banknotes for Insertion into a Cash Box

Make sure that you have checked the banknotes before you refill the Cash Box. This is necessary to prevent jammed banknotes.

Remove any banknotes that have been torn by the wrapper .
  1. Straighten crumpled banknotes, or remove them if they are badly crumpled
    2. .
  1. Fan the banknotes in order to separate individual banknote ends (both ends) and sides (both sides) 2 to 3 times each.

When loading mint banknotes, load groups of about 500 pieces, reversing each group to face left or right alternately, to prevent a stack that is much thicker on one side.

Preparing Used Banknotes for Insertion into a Cash Box

Do not refill the Cash Box with the following types of banknotes, to avoid jammed banknotes.

Banknotes clearly folded in half or in quarters
  1. Worn banknotes
  2. Torn banknotes
  3. Banknotes stuck together with adhesive tape
  4. Dog-eared banknotes

Arched banknotes should be bent back the opposite way.

Note: Load curved banknotes so that the bottom of the curve in the middle of the banknotes is near the Cash Box Pusher Plate.

  • Remove any banknotes that protrude more than 2 to 3 mm from the edge of the stack.
  • Push down the Cash Box Pusher Plate before replacing banknotes that need to be removed.
